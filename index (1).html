Introduction

Manikarnika Ghat in Varanasi is one of Hinduism's most sacred cremation grounds. Many Hindus believe that cremation here can lead to moksha (salvation). The ghat is associated with mythology (the story of Sati and the falling of her earrings, "Manikarnika") and remains an important spiritual and ritual site on the banks of the Ganges.

Key aspects

  • Moksha (Salvation): Cremation here is believed to aid liberation from the cycle of rebirth.
  • Mythological origin: Named after Mata Sati's earring (Manikarnika) in local tradition.
  • Continuous cremation: Pyres are tended day and night.
  • Two sections: Northern area often used for bathing and rituals; southern area is used for cremations.
  • Location: Situated on the Ganges between Dashashwamedh and Scindia Ghats.

What to see & do

  • Observe cremation rituals respectfully from a distance (this is a working cremation ground).
  • Witness the continuous funeral pyres and the ash — a powerful expression of the life/death cycle.
  • Watch priests perform rites and pilgrims offering prayers.
  • Experience the profound spiritual atmosphere that blends life, death, and devotion.

How to get there

  • By air: Nearest airport is Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport (VNS).
  • From the city: Take a taxi/auto to Godowlia Chowk and walk through the narrow lanes toward the ghats.
  • By boat: Hire a boat from Dashashwamedh Ghat for riverfront views of Manikarnika and surrounding ghats.

Important note: This is a sacred, working cremation ground. Maintain respect, privacy, and solemnity when visiting. Photography and approach should be sensitive to people performing last rites.
